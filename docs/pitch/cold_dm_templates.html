Cold DM / Outreach Templates

For LinkedIn targeting: Director / VP / Head of Computational Biology, Target ID, Drug Discovery
Target companies: Series B–C biotech (50–500 people) or Big Pharma innovation teams

Template 1 · Recommended First Message

Demo Offer

Hi [Name] — I built an AI platform that automates drug target triage. Mind if I show you a 15-minute demo on one of your current targets?
Hi [Name], I noticed you're leading computational biology at [Company] — looks like you're working on [disease area/program if visible on profile]. I recently built BioOrchestrator, an AI platform that takes a gene target and produces a GO/NO-GO recommendation in 15 minutes — aggregating OpenTargets, PubMed, ChEMBL, DGIdb, UniProt, and ClinicalTrials simultaneously, running 5 parallel reasoning modes, and generating a 21 CFR Part 11 compliant audit trail. I'm offering free 20-minute demos to computational biology teams this month. Happy to run it live on any target you're currently evaluating — no prep needed on your end. Worth a quick look? I can send a Calendly link. [Your name]
Best for: Cold outreach when you have done some profile research. Personalizing with their disease area doubles response rates.
Template 2 · Higher Response Rate

Problem-First

Hi [Name], Quick question: how long does your team currently spend on manual target triage before a GO/NO-GO decision? I'm asking because I built a platform that compresses that workflow from 2–4 weeks to 15 minutes — with a full audit trail. Just launched and looking for feedback from people actually doing this work. If you have 20 minutes, I'd love to run it on one of your targets live. No slides, just the tool. [Your name]
Why it works: Opens with a question — gets people thinking about their own pain. Short, no pitch until they engage. Works well for unknown prospects.
Template 3 · Warm Trigger

After They View Your LinkedIn Post

Hi [Name] — saw you viewed my post about BioOrchestrator. Would love your take on it as someone working in [their field]. Happy to do a live demo if you're curious — takes 15 minutes and I'll run it on any target you throw at me.
Timing: Send within 24 hours of seeing the view. This is your highest-converting trigger.
Template 4 · Warm Introduction

After Conference / Shared Connection

Hi [Name], [Mutual connection] suggested I reach out — I built an AI drug target intelligence platform and he thought you'd have an interesting perspective on it. The short version: it takes a gene symbol, queries 6 live databases in parallel, runs 5 LLM reasoning modes, and produces a GO/NO-GO scorecard with a GxP-compliant audit trail. Built for exactly the kind of work [Company] is doing in [disease area]. 20 minutes for a demo? I'll run it on a target of your choice.
Template 5 · Follow-Up

Send 5 Days After No Reply

Hi [Name] — just following up on my earlier message. If this isn't relevant to what you're working on right now, totally understand. But if target triage speed or regulatory documentation is a pain point on your team, I'd love to show you what BioOrchestrator produces. No commitment — just 15 minutes and a live demo.
Rule: Maximum 2 follow-ups (Day 1 + Day 5 + Day 14). After that, move on. Don't spam.

Response Handling Scripts

"Interesting, tell me more"
Send Loom link + offer Calendly for 30-min call. Keep it short — let the demo do the work.
"What does it cost?"
"For early design partners I'm offering a free 2-week pilot on 3 targets. If it adds value, we can talk about a paid engagement. No pressure — I just want feedback from people doing real target ID work."
"We already have tools for this"
"Totally makes sense. The main thing that's different is the GxP audit trail and the AI reasoning synthesis — most tools give you data, this gives you a defensible decision. Happy to show you in 15 minutes if you're ever curious how it compares."
"Send me more info"
Send the 1-page capability deck (capability_deck.html) + demo URL. Follow up in 3 days if no reply.

Target List Sources

  1. LinkedIn Sales Navigator (free trial) → search "Computational Biology" + "Biotech" + "Director OR VP OR Head"
  2. Y Combinator portfolio (ycombinator.com/companies) → filter by "Biotech" → find comp bio leads
  3. Crunchbase → filter Series B-C biotechs → find leadership on LinkedIn
  4. STAT News, FierceBiotech job postings → companies actively hiring comp bio = active target ID programs
  5. AACR / Bio-IT World attendee lists (often public)
