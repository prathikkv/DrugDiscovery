## THE PROBLEM

Drug target selection is the highest-stakes, most manual step in pharmaceutical R&D.

A typical computational biology team spends **2–4 weeks** manually triaging a single target — bouncing between 6+ disconnected databases, assembling evidence in spreadsheets, before presenting a GO/NO-GO recommendation to leadership.

**Wrong decisions here cost $100M–$2.6B and years of failed development.**
90% of drugs that enter Phase I trials fail. The leading cause: wrong target selection.

---

## THE SOLUTION

BioOrchestrator compresses 2–4 weeks of manual work into **15 minutes**.

```
Gene Symbol  →  Evidence  →  AI Reasoning  →  Scoring  →  GO/NO-GO
               (6 DBs)       (5 LLM modes)   (7 dims)    + Audit Trail
```

**Evidence Sources (queried simultaneously):**
OpenTargets · DGIdb · PubMed · UniProt · ChEMBL · ClinicalTrials.gov

**AI Reasoning Modes (run in parallel):**
Hypothesis Generation · Evidence Synthesis · Contradiction Detection · Gap Analysis · Confidence Assessment

**Scoring Dimensions:**
Genetic Evidence · Druggability · Safety & Selectivity · Expression Biology ·
Competitive Landscape · Clinical Translation · Literature Consensus

**Compliance:**
21 CFR Part 11 compliant SHA-256 hash-chain audit trail with electronic signatures.
Human-in-the-loop approval gates at every critical decision point.
