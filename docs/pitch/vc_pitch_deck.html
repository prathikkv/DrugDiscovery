Appendix — Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why can't Big Pharma just build this internally?
Internal tools take 18–36 months, cost $2M+ to build, and still need GxP validation. A validated SaaS product with an audit trail is cheaper and faster. Same reason they buy Benchling instead of building their ELN.
Q: What's stopping Illumina or Thermo Fisher from copying this?
They focus on wet-lab instruments and sequencing tools, not AI reasoning layers. They'd acquire, not build. That's an exit path.
Q: Do you need proprietary data to be useful?
No — runs entirely on public databases and customer-uploaded data. Proprietary data integration is a paid enterprise tier.
Q: What's the regulatory risk of AI in pharma?
The audit trail and HITL gates address this. AI produces evidence synthesis and scoring — a human scientist makes the final GO/NO-GO decision and signs off. Analogous to AI-assisted radiology: AI flags, human decides.
Q: Why is GxP compliance a moat?
It's not a feature — it's a barrier to entry. Getting into a regulated pharma workflow requires validated software (IQ/OQ/PQ) which takes months and requires specific documentation. We built the framework (VMP, URS, FRS, FMEA, SOPs) from day one. Competitors would need to retrofit this.
