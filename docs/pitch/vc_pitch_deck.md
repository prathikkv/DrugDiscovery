## APPENDIX — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why can't Big Pharma just build this internally?**
A: They can, and some do — but internal tools take 18–36 months, cost $2M+ to build, and still need GxP validation. A validated SaaS product with an audit trail is cheaper and faster. The same reason they buy Benchling instead of building their ELN.

**Q: What's stopping Illumina or Thermo Fisher from copying this?**
A: They focus on wet-lab instruments and sequencing tools, not AI reasoning layers. They'd acquire, not build. That's an exit path.

**Q: Do you need proprietary data to be useful?**
A: No — the platform runs entirely on public databases and customer-uploaded data. Proprietary data integration (internal compound libraries, in-house omics) is a paid enterprise tier.

**Q: What's the regulatory risk of AI in pharma?**
A: The audit trail and HITL gates are specifically designed to address this. The AI produces evidence synthesis and scoring — a human scientist makes the final GO/NO-GO decision and signs off with an e-signature. This is analogous to how AI-assisted radiology works: AI flags, human decides.

**Q: Why is GxP compliance a moat?**
A: It's not a feature — it's a barrier to entry. To get into a regulated pharma workflow, software must be validated. That validation process (IQ/OQ/PQ) takes months and requires specific documentation. We built the documentation framework (VMP, URS, FRS, FMEA, SOPs) from day one. Competitors would need to retrofit this.
